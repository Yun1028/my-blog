## Overview

During the HRL experiments, I noticed that the robot on the first map behaved differently from the robots on the other maps.

At first, I interpreted the problem as a limitation of exploration or policy structure.
However, after checking the observation pipeline more carefully, I found that the real issue was in the terrain input.

## What I Found

At the time, I assumed that raycasting was effectively handled per robot, so that each robot would read terrain from the map it was actually placed on.

After checking the setup more carefully, I found that this was not what was happening in my implementation.
While going back through the official documentation, I also realized that I had misunderstood this part of the raycasting setup.

In practice, all robots were receiving terrain observations based on a single map, which meant that the environments other than the first one were not getting the correct terrain input.

As a result, the earlier failure was not mainly caused by exploration difficulty or model structure.
The more fundamental issue was that training had been performed with incorrectly connected terrain observations.

---

## What Changed After That

Once I identified the input issue, I did not continue pushing the HRL structure further for this problem.

Instead, I went back to the earlier standard RL codebase, adjusted the map setup, and retrained the policy.
After that correction, the learning behavior became much more normal, and the previously blocked behaviors were resolved much more directly.
