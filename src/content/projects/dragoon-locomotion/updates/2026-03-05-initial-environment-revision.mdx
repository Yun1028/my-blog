## Why I Started This Project

I had long wanted to train a four-legged robot.
I often watch StarCraft, and the Dragoon unit especially stood out to me because its movement style does not really exist in nature.
It has a very unusual four-legged structure, and I wanted to see whether I could actually build something inspired by it
and make it walk in simulation.

This project became my first full reinforcement learning practice project:
from concept design, to 3D asset creation, to physics setup, and finally to Isaac Sim.

I used GPT to refine the concept I wanted, then used a 3D generation service to create a model from that idea.
After that, I imported the generated mesh into Blender.

However, the generated 3D model could not be used directly for simulation.

A purely visual mesh is not enough for Isaac Sim.
To make the asset usable in physics-based reinforcement learning, I had to build the physical structure required by simulation,
including collision meshes and articulation-related information.

After preparing the structure in Blender and exporting it into a usable format,
I imported the robot into Isaac Sim.

This was the first point where the project began to feel like a real reinforcement learning system rather than just a design experiment.
The asset could now be placed in simulation, inspected as an articulated body,
and prepared for later stages such as environment design, reward shaping, and locomotion training.

---

## Final Note

This update represents the starting point of the Dragoon project.

What mattered most here was not training performance yet, but building the entire pipeline myself:
starting from an idea, turning it into a 3D asset, preparing the physics structure,
and bringing it into Isaac Sim as the foundation for reinforcement learning experiments.
